# Lore Discovery Pass — SCP Series I & K-Class Framing

## Series I as material

Series I (001–999) predates the modern wiki. Early entries originated on 4chan and the EditThis predecessor, migrated to Wikidot in July 2008, and several lost their original authorship in the move. Consequence: Series I has **high, visible quality variance** — some entries are foundational, many are crude or stereotyped. This is not a defect to design around; it is diegetic cover. A site running on a deprecated OS, with files of inconsistent rigor and uncertain provenance, is exactly where redaction gaps, contradictory cross-references, and corruptible records are *plausible*. The unreliability of the Series I document is a feature you inherit for free.

## K-class taxonomy — pick the right prefix

The entity's stated goal (already-achieved localized K-class, attempting a site-wide breach) maps onto the established prefix system. Do not default to XK.

- **XK** — broadest "end of world." 103+ SCPs use it. Too generic to be load-bearing; reads as filler.
- **ZK** — reality fundamentally broken or eliminated, usually via physics. Terminal, not incremental.
- **CK** — reality *restructuring*: an entity alters history broadly **or incrementally**. This is the keystone. Incremental retroactive alteration of the record *is* your propagation mechanic. The localized K-class the entity already caused = it has restructured the site's record of reality by hiding inside the OS. The site-wide goal = scaling that restructuring until containment records no longer match contained reality.
- **NK** (gray goo), **SK** (dominance shift), **VK** (localized dimensional collapse) — secondary flavor for individual breached entities.

Frame the entity as a **CK-class causer operating through information**, not a monster behind a door. Its weapon is the edit. Containment fails not because a wall breaks but because the *files describing the walls* stop being true.

## Thematic veins to mine

1. **Containment-as-bureaucracy.** The horror is clinical detachment over an abyss. Clearance tiers, addenda, incident logs. The terminal UI is the genre.
2. **Redaction-as-epistemics.** Clearance gating means knowledge is rationed by authority. Your access-level loop is already pure Foundation.
3. **Information/memetic/cognitohazards.** Anomalies that act through perception, memory, and the document itself. The entity belongs to this class — an infohazard living in the record. This category is your curation target.
4. **The unreliable document.** Series I's variance lets corruption read as ambiguous: is this file wrong because the entity edited it, or because it was always badly written? Productive doubt.

## Curation, grounded in lore

Reaffirming last turn's foreknowledge constraint with thematic backing:

- **Favor info/memetic/perceptual anomalies and obscure entries.** They serve the entity's nature and resist player foreknowledge.
- **The "Mister" set is a ready-made dense graph.** Mr. Forgetful (909), Mr. Hungry (913), Mr. Moon (917), Mr. Lost (920), Mr. Sillybug (955) and others share a naming convention and recurring-character status — natural cross-reference edges, low foreknowledge, thematically tied to memory/perception/loss. Strong anchor cluster.
- **Avoid 173 / 096 / 682.** Foreknowledge kills decipherment on exactly the headline entries.
