## 2. Arc summaries

### 2.0 SCP-055 — the archetype

The founding object: a thing in a vault that cannot be remembered. Observers retain only **negations** — "it isn't round, it isn't green" — and must re-deduce its existence every visit, in a debriefing scene that loops on itself. The document *performs* its anomaly: information presented and perpetually slipping away. No guards are posted, because guards would forget what they guard. An addendum floats the unresolvable question of whether 055 is a planted enemy agent — a mystery the anomaly itself protects.

**Take:** knowing-by-negation as a puzzle shape; the document that demonstrates its own subject; containment procedures that read as absurd until you understand them — then read as desperate.

### 2.1 There Is No Antimemetics Division (the Marion era)

The Division fights ideas that eat the evidence of themselves. Its chief, Marion Wheeler, runs on memory-retention drugs ("mnestics") because her job cannot otherwise be remembered; the title is the Division's own security posture — even most of the Foundation doesn't know it exists.

- **We Need To Talk About Fifty-Five** — Wheeler re-orients an O5 whose missed medication let an antimemetic "assistant" embed beside him for years. The horror is administrative: the predator's camouflage is *being unremarkable on the org chart*.
- **Introductory Antimemetics** — a "new researcher's first day" that turns out to be a veteran's stripped memory; the predator lives in the site's lower levels and in the database itself, and its containment entry is *written and maintained by its victims*. "Ideas don't die."
- **Unforgettable, That's What You Are** — the Division's founding is a lie of necessity: an earlier incarnation (a WWII-era "Unthinkables" unit) met something that eats knowledge, and chose collective self-amnesia as the only working containment. The Division is *looped* — it has died and forgotten itself before. Survival at the end means deliberately wiping your own mind.
- **CASE COLOURLESS GREEN** — Wheeler, in a shielded room, watches a video of her own prior self explaining the thing she will be forced to re-forget: a vast hostile idea-complex that kills anyone who comprehends it, and has already quietly eliminated nearly everyone who ever studied it. The room is the only place the knowledge can exist.
- **Your Last First Day** — the breach. The idea-complex (SCP-3125) incarnates inside Site-41; Wheeler descends through her compromised site, past colleagues who are no longer themselves, to a contingency bunker, and erases the entire site — herself included — with an antimemetic warhead. "Ideas can be killed. — How? — With better ideas." Containment by self-deletion, again, one level up.

**Arc shape:** institution → revelation that the institution has already lost before → sacrifice that no one will be allowed to remember. Each story is a cold open into a state of ignorance the previous story explained.

### 2.2 SCP-2256 — the interlude elegy

Kilometer-tall, nearly weightless creatures walking the Pacific horizon, naturally antimemetic; only one dying Polynesian tradition could see them ("the ones who walk very slowly"). Foundation *observation itself* kills them — attention is the toxin — and after the last one dies, the data follows: detailed records decay fastest, broad descriptions linger, the document fills with rot as you read down the page. Discovery → fascination → complicity → extinction → the file forgetting its own subject.

**Take:** the record as a second, slower casualty; grief encoded as data decay; precision as fragility (the more exactly you state a thing, the faster it dies). The strongest single model for our decaying-OS aesthetic.

### 2.3 Five Five Five Five Five (the Adam era)

SCP-3125 — a five-dimensional predatory idea-complex, signature five-fold/pentagonal imagery, an idea so large it kills its comprehenders — wins. The world ends and **nobody perceives that it ended.** The protagonist is a civilian: Adam Wheeler, a violinist, Marion's husband, erased from her life by her own hand to keep him off 3125's map ("Where Have You Been All My Life": she discovers him, verifies him, and orders the erasure — "My husband's dead").

- **Fresh Hell** is the flashback of how they met: Adam stumbles into an anomaly response and meets operative Marion Hutchinson — competent, amused, mid-"blizzard" of anomalies. Ordinary love embedded retroactively in a doomed timeline.
- **Ojai** shows the spread: a willing host streaming the idea outward; the responding agent's compassion is the wrong tool, because the host *chose* merger.
- **CASE HATE RED** — the apocalypse from a civilian seat: mid-concert, Adam alone perceives that everyone has been replaced; black vertical rectangles, wrong language, humanity "corrected." His protection — Marion's erasure of him — has become his solitude.
- **Ará Orún / Unthreaded / Blood/Brain** — Adam, maimed (severed fingers — the violinist's loss) and guided by the last Foundation voices (operative Daisy Ulrich; later automated systems), crosses the dead world to Site-41's crater. Marion persists only as residue: a chalk drawing, a corrupted ghost in the noösphere "like a Swiss watch filled with glue," a body resting on the warhead switch. The guides die off-screen, unwitnessed.
- **Wild Light / Tombstone** — the countermeasure: memeticist Bart Hughes, sealed in a vault for decades, rebuilding himself into a living idea-engine, lacks only a "seed idea" worth weaponizing. The seed is Marion — not her body, the *idea of her*, reconstructed from Adam's recovered memories. She ascends into ideatic space and unmakes 3125 by pure negation, departing as "a final wild photon, outbound to the deepest limit of ideatic space, never to return."
- **Champions of Nothing** — the epilogue indicts the survivors: a year of history and millions of people are simply *gone*, and the Foundation cannot remember whether it fought well, fought at all, or deserves to have survived. "Humans can walk away from, and forget, anything." The last image belongs to an animal that evolved antimemetic armor *against the Foundation's own attention* — life adapting beyond institutional sight.

**Arc shape:** the institutional lens is discarded; a civilian inherits the war; love functions as the only data structure that survives erasure; victory is total and totally unwitnessed.

### 2.4 What the Dead Know (the revenant era)

Dead operatives persist as informational ghosts ("Saints") anchored to the living by identity bonds, fighting in the noösphere. Doctrine is explicit and listable — five dogmas of identity defense (Narrative, Anchor, Mission, Emotion, Society) — turning selfhood into fortification. SCP-2111's document is itself a compartmentalized directory of offset files, access-gated, the format mimicking a filesystem. The arc runs concurrent with the 3125 war: the dead hold the line at Site-41's erasure; later they hunt for Hughes, hiding at a site that no longer exists.

**Take:** the afterlife as records-continuity; identity as a defensible structure with named load-bearing parts; **the document formatted as an interface** (directory trees, offset files) — directly adjacent to our fake-OS frame.

## 3. Themes (cross-canon synthesis)

1. **Knowledge is exposure.** Comprehension is the attack surface; to understand the threat is to be targeted by it. (Our inference-is-the-spend invariant is this theme mechanized.)
2. **The institution cannot know itself.** The Division's perfect containment and its perfect failure are indistinguishable from inside; founding documents are cover stories; the org has died before and runs on the residue.
3. **Sacrifice without witness.** Every major act of heroism is paid for with the memory of it — the inverse of legacy. The dead are not remembered; at best they are *used* (Marion as warhead payload).
4. **Attention as violence.** Observation kills 2256; precision decays fastest; filing something can be the act that destroys it.
5. **Love as the durable record.** The only information structures that survive erasure are relational — a marriage, an anchor, a chalk drawing. Bureaucratic memory fails; personal memory, imperfect and partial, is the redundant backup.
6. **The cyclical institution.** Loops, re-foundings, first days that are not first days. History isn't lost once; it is lost *on a schedule*.
7. **The unperceived apocalypse.** The world can end without an event; catastrophe as subtraction rather than spectacle.

## 4. Symbols & motifs catalog

- **The retention pill** — chemically rented memory; consciousness with a dosage schedule and a failure mode.
- **Knowing-by-negation** — inventories of what a thing is *not*; deduction from an absence-shaped hole.
- **Notes-to-self as lifeline** — ink on skin, videos from your prior self, entries maintained by victims: the self as an unreliable colleague you must brief in writing.
- **Five-fold geometry** — the enemy's watermark recurring in hands, points, pentagons; a *count* as a corruption signature.
- **The empty workstation** — staff who were never there, departments that answer no requisitions, an org chart with load-bearing vacancies.
- **Data rot as wound** — files that decay legibly, detail-first; redaction not as censorship but as *injury*.
- **The descending elevator / the bunker** — truth stored at depth, under the institution's floor.
- **The severed fingers** — capability loss made permanent and bodily; the musician who cannot play the self he was.
- **The chalk drawing / the ghost in the noösphere** — a person surviving only as low-fidelity representation.
- **The new star / the wild photon** — victory legible only as a distant, uninterpretable light.
- **The black rectangle** — the redaction bar escaped from the page into the world.

## 5. Structural devices catalog

- **The document performs its anomaly** (055, 2256): form enacts content — our entire game premise generalized.
- **Cold-open amnesia**: each installment begins inside an ignorance the previous installment explained; re-orientation is the recurring scene.
- **The looped briefing**: a character (and reader) re-derives the same horror from negative space, twice, with the second derivation hitting harder.
- **Document-as-interface** (2111): filesystem mimicry, offset files, access errors as narrative beats.
- **The victim-maintained record**: documentation whose authorship is itself the horror.
- **Witness inversion**: climaxes deliberately staged where no surviving observer exists; the epilogue inspects the hole instead of the event.

## 6. What Site-41B inherits

Site-41B is *not* the Antimemetics Division and ships none of its canon. What crosses the licensing wall is structural:

1. **The pre-mnestic premise — our original ground.** The hub's Division fights with chemical memory armor. Site-41B is the *sister site that never got the armor*: an annex fighting informational anomalies **blind**, with paperwork as its only prosthetic memory. Every motif above gets refracted through bureaucracy-as-mnemonic-ritual: carbon copies in triplicate because three copies decay slower than one; cross-referencing as a survival practice, not a filing convention. This inversion is original, era-appropriate to our Series-I register, and *explains our game mechanics diegetically* — a site that knows its records are its mind builds exactly the kind of cross-linked, redundancy-obsessed corpus the player explores.
2. **The record as the second casualty** (from 2256): our decaying OS is the elegy form — files degrade detail-first under exposure.
3. **Knowing-by-negation** (from 055): the inference surface for high-tier anchors — prose that catalogs what the redacted thing is not.
4. **The org-chart hole** (from the WNTTAF predator + empty-workstation motif): staff and departments whose absence is structured into the documents — feeds our recurring-cast design.
5. **Witnessless sacrifice + the cyclical institution**: the predecessor-archivist arc; the site has run this loop before, and the player's role is the loop's current iteration.
6. **Document-as-interface** (from 2111): license to make the OS itself narrate — directory structures, access errors, and validation messages as story beats.
7. **The unperceived apocalypse as endgame register** (from the Adam era): our CK-class restructuring ending is subtraction, not spectacle — the record quietly replacing the world.

What does **not** cross: the Division, mnestics by that name, any character, SCP-055/2256/3125/2111 and their resolutions, five-fold imagery as an enemy signature (too identifying), and qntm's specific coinages. Site-41B's terminology, cast, anomalies, and resolutions are authored fresh in `site_41b.md`.
