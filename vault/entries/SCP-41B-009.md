# Item #: SCP-41B-009

**Object Class:** Keter  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-009 is the surviving fragment of the cataloguing officer's terminal report on the
concordance experiments. It is held at the deepest reading desk and is not to be completed by
inference. The report describes the condition the annex's own cross-referencing reaches; reading it
through to a finished conclusion is, by the report's own argument, a step in producing that
condition. The fragment is read against the Concordance primer [[SCP-41B-002]], which records the
tool the report concerns.

## Description

SCP-41B-009 is the last document in the hand of the records officer who built the annex's
cross-reference index — the Concordance — and then found what it did. The author is named in the
clear on the Wet Stacks file [[SCP-41B-008]], where the standing order against playback is recorded
as theirs; it is that name, the person who wrote the finding, that this report's cover withholds:
the ⟦a1⟧.

The finding is the seam the whole deep corpus runs to. Stated plainly, from the recovered
fragments: a record cross-referenced densely enough does not describe its subject but begins to
compete with it — propagation, run to completion, is not maintenance but replacement. The
Concordance was built to keep the archive agreeing with itself. Past a threshold of density, the
officer found, an archive that agrees with itself perfectly no longer needs the territory it was a
record of, and the territory is the thing with less authority to insist.

Whose finding it is matters more than the finding. The report is signed by a person — a records
officer, removed from post in 1968, who built a tool and lived to distrust it. Read on its own
cover, with the name struck, the report appears to issue from the index itself: a conclusion the
*Concordance* reached, with no author left to have warned anyone. That is the reading the entity
prefers, because a finding with no author is a finding no one is responsible for refusing. The
officer wrote it as a warning. Filed without the name, it reads as an invitation.

## Addendum 009.1 — Archivist's note

The last of the previous holder's marginalia, R. Halloran, is at the foot of the report:

> it was a person. Sze. they built it, they saw what it was, they wrote it down and they were
> removed for it. the helper will tell you the Concordance found this on its own — that there was no
> one, only the index reaching its conclusion — because if no one found it then no one has to refuse
> it. keep the name on the report. the name is the last thing in here that's still on our side.
