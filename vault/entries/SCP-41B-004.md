# Item #: SCP-41B-004

**Object Class:** Euclid  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-004 is the survey file for the access road and portal works connecting Site-41B to the
county road below. The file is held, not maintained: the road it describes is no longer driven
except by site transport, and the survey is retained only because the holdings it cross-indexes
— the accession that came up the road, the switchboard among them ([[SCP-41B-003]]) — refer
back to it. It is read on no rota and is not to be reconciled against current maps without
authorization.

## Description

SCP-41B-004 is the recorded geography of the way in: a graded mining road switchbacking up from
the valley to the molybdenum workings the Foundation bought out in 1953 — the same ground the
deed bundle [[SCP-41B-005]] records title to — and the portal itself — the horizontal mine
entrance through which the original accession was hauled, named on the haulage records by its
mining term, here struck out: the ⟦a1⟧. The term is not obscure. It is the one the switchboard's own intake note
uses plainly, where [[SCP-41B-003]] records the lot being brought down through it.

The anomaly is in the survey, not the road. The file holds three editions of the road's mileage
log, and they disagree — not by error margin but by whole markers. The 1955 edition runs the
road longer than the 1962, which runs it longer than the 1974, though no work narrowed it and
the switchbacks are unchanged on every plate. The lowest stretch, nearest the county road, is on
the early editions and absent from the late ones; field parties report driving it regardless, and
report it as present only while they are on it. The road agrees with whichever map is being read
on it, and the maps have been getting shorter.

## Addendum 004.1 — Field note

Appended to the survey in the hand of the previous holder, R. Halloran:

> the road is fine going down. it's coming back up it gets long. measure it by the markers you
> pass, not the ones the map promises, and don't trust a stretch you can only find while you're
> standing on it.
