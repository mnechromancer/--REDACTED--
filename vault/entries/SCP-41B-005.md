# Item #: SCP-41B-005

**Object Class:** Safe  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-005 is the legal-records bundle for the land the annex sits under: the patented mining
claim, its deed history, and the 1953 instrument by which the Foundation acquired the works. It
is filed flat in the deed safe on Stack I and cross-indexed to the access survey [[SCP-41B-004]],
which describes the ground these papers say who owns. The bundle is read on no rota and is to be
produced only against a records request that cites it by claim number.

## Description

SCP-41B-005 is the deed history of a patented claim worked, until it played out, for a single
metal — the ore the survey file names where it describes the workings the road climbs to, here
struck from the deed: the ⟦a1⟧. The metal is named plainly on [[SCP-41B-004]]; the claim that
this bundle records is the claim on that same ground, and the word the deed withholds is the word
the survey prints.

The acquisition instrument itself is ordinary and countersigned: the 1953 purchase was authorized
over the signature of the site director of record, I. Vogel, whose office also signs the supply
orders the inner departments draw on ([[SCP-41B-006]]). The anomaly is in the chain of title
before that signature, not in the claim. The deed runs backward through ordinary hands — a mining
company, a pair of prospectors, a territorial grant — until, three conveyances before the grant,
it does not stop. The earliest instrument in the bundle conveys the claim *to*
its first recorded owner from a grantor named only by a registry mark, on a date that predates
the land office that recorded it. The claim, in other words, was filed as already owned, by a
holder the office that filed it could not have seated. What the deed establishes is not who took
the land but that the land was on file before anyone is recorded reaching it.

## Addendum 005.1 — Archivist's note

A note in the hand of the previous holder, R. Halloran, is paper-clipped to the earliest deed:

> read the deeds up, not down. somewhere near the bottom the paper starts owning the ground
> instead of recording who does. don't let the helper tidy that conveyance out; it's the one
> that matters.
