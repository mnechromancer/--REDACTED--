# Containment Amendment — Stack II Holding

**FORM 41-C · SITE-41B DEEP-RECORDS ANNEX**

**HOLDING:** The bound register of shaft-work calls — the call-and-answer verses the mine
crews used below the third gallery, transcribed at conversion and held on Stack II. One
volume, ordinary paper, ordinary ink.

**SECTION AMENDED:** Special containment procedures, handling and reading.

**OBSERVED BEHAVIOUR:** The register propagates by comprehension. Staff who have read the
verses and taken their sense report the calls recurring in their own speech within the
audit month — first as habit, then as answer, the staff member supplying the response
verse to calls no one has made. Staff who shelve, stamp, re-bind, or carry the same
volume without reading it are unaffected without exception, and the annex has moved it
freely on this basis for years. Effect follows understanding, not exposure; a clerk who
memorized two verses phonetically, in a language the clerk does not hold, remains clear.

**HAZARD TYPE OF RECORD:** Reclassified ⟦a1⟧, per the definitions fixed by circular. The
prior entry, a general cognitohazard stamp applied at conversion, is void as recording
nothing; the caution was correct and useless. The reclassifying officer states the
behaviour above and lets the type follow from it, per the circular's instruction.

**EFFECTIVE READING:** The register passes to the no-read list. It may be filed by any
hand and read by none; the reading rota drops it; audits count its pages without taking
their sense. The annex is aware that counting pages without reading them is a discipline,
and maintains it as one.

> difference between a caution and a countermeasure. learn the word, not the stamp.
