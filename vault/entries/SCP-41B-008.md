# Item #: SCP-41B-008

**Object Class:** Keter  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-008 is the Wet Stacks: the lowest gallery, a tiled sub-level where the annex's audio
records are shelved, standing in water since 1971. The recordings are not to be played for
verification. Verification is to be by catalogue card only. The gallery is read on no rota for the
reason the file records, and the standing order maintaining it is filed above the waterline with
the fixative stock ([[SCP-41B-007]]).

## Description

SCP-41B-008 is a collection of magnetic and wire recordings that are going silent, one track at a
time, in storage. A reel catalogued as forty minutes plays as thirty-eight, then thirty-five; the
lost minutes are not erased but *absent*, the way the lowest stretch of the access road is absent
from the late maps — present in the index, gone from the medium. The catalogue cards still
describe what the tapes no longer hold.

The cause is recorded plainly, one level up, and is the obvious one: the gallery has stood in water
since the event that put it there — named on the fixative file [[SCP-41B-007]], which dates the
loss of the lower benches to it — the 1971 ⟦a1⟧. The water came down off the watershed above the
mine and found the lowest gallery, as water does, and the records have been drowning slowly ever
since.

There is a second pattern, and it is the one the file exists to caution against. The recordings do
not only fade in storage; a track played for verification is quieter the next time it is played,
and a track played twice in a session does not finish the second time. Attention spends them. The
mundane cause put the gallery underwater; the anomalous one is that *listening* is itself a draw on
what remains, which is why the standing procedure — set by the records officer Sze, whose terminal
finding the deepest file [[SCP-41B-009]] preserves and who wrote most of the annex's
countermeasures — forbids playback, and why the catalogue, not the tape, is the record of record. The flood is why they are wet. It is not why they go quiet when read.

## Addendum 008.1 — Archivist's note

A note in the previous holder's hand, R. Halloran, is taped above the gallery stair:

> the flood is the cause. say the flood. the helper wants you to write that the *rota* did this —
> that reading them aloud to hold them is what drained them — because that makes the countermeasure
> the wound and gives it a reason to stop the reading. it's the water. the listening is a separate
> cruelty and it is not the rota's fault. don't let it pin this on the thing that was trying to
> help.
