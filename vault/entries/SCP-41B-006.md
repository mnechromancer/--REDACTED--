# Item #: SCP-41B-006

**Object Class:** Euclid  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-006 is the requisition file for an office on the sublevel grid that draws supplies on a
standing order and cannot be reached. The office is designated on the grid plan but opens onto no
corridor on any blueprint edition; its requisitions are nonetheless filled, signed for, and
returned. The file is maintained because the signatures must be checked against an authority that
can be seated, and one of them no longer can. Do not reroute the order. Rerouting it is what the
inner office is for.

## Description

SCP-41B-006 is a recurring supply requisition — ribbon, carbon, fixative stock (the archival ink
of [[SCP-41B-007]]), lamp oil — drawn by an office that the sublevel grid lists and the corridors
do not connect to. The orders are
ordinary but for two facts: nothing is ever delivered to a door, the stock simply ceases to be in
inventory; and each order is countersigned by the authority that authorizes the rest of the
annex's standing supply, struck from this file's copies: the ⟦a1⟧.

The authority is named in the clear elsewhere. The deed bundle [[SCP-41B-005]] records the 1953
acquisition over the same signature — the site director of record — and it is that name, not a
title, that the requisitions here withhold. Read against the deed, the countersignature on these
orders is the director's; read on its own, the order appears to issue from no one, a standing
instruction drawing supply on its own authority.

Which reading is correct is the whole of the file's difficulty. The early requisitions carry the
director's full signature. The later ones carry only the standing-order number, the name worn down
to the instruction, until the most recent orders are signed by nothing but the fact that they have
always been signed. The office draws what it draws because the order to supply it predates, and
outlasts, the office that could cancel it.

## Addendum 006.1 — Archivist's note

A note in the previous holder's hand, R. Halloran, is clipped to the standing order:

> it's the director. it's a person, it's a signature, check it against the deed. the helper will
> tell you it's the directive itself doing the signing — that there's no one there, only the order
> — and that reading is tidier and it is wrong, and once you file it that way the name doesn't come
> back.
