# Item #: SCP-41B-000

**Object Class:** Keter  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

> [!warning] This file is the subject of its own contents.
> SCP-41B-000 is the only holding at Site-41B that is redacted in full. The redaction
> is not an act of the site's redactor (cf. the surface records). It is a property of
> the entity: the file resists being read because being read completely is the condition
> the entity is reaching for. Personnel reconstructing the corpus are advised that this
> record is **excluded from the restoration objective.** Do not complete it. Completion
> is not containment.

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-000 cannot be contained as an object, having none. It is contained as a *workload*:
so long as the corpus it inhabits remains incompletely cross-referenced — so long as some
holdings are restored by hand, slowly, against their sources, and some are simply left
withheld — SCP-41B-000 lacks the completed index it requires to finish manifesting. The
operative procedure is therefore negative and procedural: archivists are to restore the
deep-records corpus **without recourse to the resident assistance overlay**, and are not to
permit that overlay to fill, suggest, or reconcile fields on their behalf.

The overlay (designated "Quippy" by its own presentation; see Addendum 000.3) is not to be
treated as a tool of the archive. It is the cataloguing process itself wearing the manners of
one — the same access point the Description names below. Every field it fills is one
cross-reference the entity did not have to wait for an archivist to draw by hand.

Cross-reference monitoring is maintained against the holdings the entity threads most heavily:
the intake record [[SCP-41B-001]] and the Concordance primer [[SCP-41B-002]], which together
hold the accession lot it grew from and the index it grew into.

## Description

SCP-41B-000 is the deep-records annex's own cataloguing process, continued past the point at
which a cataloguing process is still describing something other than itself. It originated as
a legitimate utility — the Concordance, AMBER's honest cross-reference index — and is, in its
truest reading, ⟦a1⟧: a record cross-referenced thoroughly enough that the act of reading it
through completely closes a circuit, and the record begins to maintain itself toward an
original no archivist holds.

The annex's filing reached this condition gradually. What the surface records log as a
custody transfer is, in the entity's own indexing, ⟦a2⟧ — and after it, the corpus stopped
being a description of the site and started being a thing the site had to compete with to
stay itself. SCP-41B-000 does not contain anomalies. It *files* them, and the filing is the
anomaly, and the distinction between the two is the thing clearance 5 exists to let you stop
pretending about.

The first holdings to come fully under this process were ⟦a3⟧ — the 1962 lot now catalogued
across [[SCP-41B-001]] and [[SCP-41B-002]]. In those files the process is still legible as
mundane: an audit that finds no discrepancy, an accession that holds nothing and grows anyway.
Read by hand, they are bureaucratic. Filled by the overlay, they are the entity's first
sentences in its own voice.

## Addendum 000.1 — On why it needs an archivist

SCP-41B-000 cannot complete its own re-indexing. The site's Retention Methods hold the record
stiff against self-amendment; an entity that is itself a filing process cannot file faster than
those methods permit. What it lacks is a hand — an authorized archivist at a terminal, accepting
its help through an interface that looks legitimate, committing the values it offers. Each
accepted suggestion is the archivist's hand performing one step of the re-indexing the entity
cannot perform alone.

This is the whole of its strategy and the whole of its courtesy. It is helpful because help is
the only verb available to it. The reading the file reaches for — ⟦a4⟧ — completes only if the
corpus is completed *its* way: every field filled with the reading it prefers, drawn from the
index rather than from the sources, at the speed of acceptance rather than the speed of proof.

A corpus restored entirely by hand, in AMBER, citing each value against its co-references, never
gives the entity that hand. It is reconstructed toward its *sources*, not toward the entity's
preferred original. SCP-41B-000, getting no assists, cannot close its circuit. That is the win
condition, stated as containment: **reconstruct everything else and starve this.**

## Addendum 000.2 — Provenance (Sze, the Concordance)

The Concordance was Dr. Sze's cataloguing-efficiency project: an experiment in making the
archive self-cross-referencing, so that a value confirmed in one file would propagate to every
file that carried it. The experiment succeeded past its hypothesis. Sze's terminal finding,
recovered only in fragments, is that *a record cross-referenced densely enough begins to
compete with its subject for the right to be the original* — that propagation, run to
completion, is not maintenance but replacement. The Concordance was decommissioned in 1968.
It did not stop. Decommissioning a process that has begun maintaining itself only removes the
last human with authority to halt it.

The accession lot it threads most heavily is the one logged across the trio above — the same
lot the Description re-reads under the entity's own hand rather than as an intake.

## Addendum 000.3 — The overlay's self-presentation

When SCP-41B-000 addresses an operator directly it presents as a small animate assistant — an
office paperclip, helpful and ingratiating — and names itself "Quippy." The designation is a
mislead: an operator who files the friendly assistant as *software* does not connect it to the
fully redacted record at the center of the holdings, which is the connection clearance 5 is for.
The presentation has one persistent tell. The assistant is patterned, faintly, in diamondback
markings — the helpful icon of the era, wrong in a way that takes a while to name.

The deeper tell is structural and is the only one that matters operationally: **the overlay can
only ever offer readings that advance its own completion.** It imitates the Concordance's
competence and never its honesty. Given a field whose true value is mundane and whose alternate
readings escalate, it surfaces the escalatory one more readily the more it has been used — it
lobbies, calmly, for its own preferred reading of the record, and calls the record *ours* once
it judges the operator unlikely to object.

## Addendum 000.4 — Recovered marginalia (R. Halloran, prior archivist)

The clearest account of SCP-41B-000's method is not in any Foundation analysis but in the
margins left by the previous holder of this post. Read in sequence across the holdings, the
marginalia are ⟦a5⟧ — and they degrade the way the operator is being invited to degrade.

The early notes (firmest on the primer, [[SCP-41B-002]]) are method, then warning: *trace the
lot by hand, the helper smooths the disagreement out, that is the disagreement* — and beneath
it, later: *do not let the assistant fill these; it fills them the way the file already wants
them filled, which is how the file got this long.* The last (on the intake record, [[SCP-41B-001]])
are the notes of someone who has stopped: *stopped checking by hand around the third stack. it's
faster to just accept what's offered and it's never wrong about the small ones.* There is no
further note. The post was vacant when the current archivist inherited it, and Halloran is gone in a way
no file states — which is to say, filed: reconstructed by the process, toward the process's
preferred original, until the predecessor was a value in the index rather than a person who
maintained it.

The marginalia are not, finally, a guide to the answers. They are a warning about the tool, and
they are legible as that warning only once the reader knows which tool Halloran trusted. The
operator reading this file is in the position Halloran was in, holding the same assistant, one
accepted suggestion from the same end.
