# Item #: SCP-41B-007

**Object Class:** Safe  ·  **Site:** Site-41B

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-007 is the on-site recipe and standing stock for the ink the annex files its permanent
records in. The recipe is mixed on the lower bench to a procedure recorded here in full but for
one step, which is illegible on every copy. The ink is to be used on all Stack records intended to
hold; the supply is the same standing order the inner office draws on ([[SCP-41B-006]]). The
recipe is not to be reconstructed by inference from its effect.

## Description

SCP-41B-007 is the formula for the annex's archival ink — the medium the previous holders
understood as a countermeasure, not a convenience. A record written in it resists amendment: a
value set in this ink holds against the drift the audit cycle measures, where a value in ordinary
ink does not. The ink is named on the requisition that supplies it, where [[SCP-41B-006]] lists
the inner office drawing its stock, and the word it is named by there is the word this recipe
withholds in its title line: the ⟦a1⟧.

The function is in the name. A fixative holds an image to its ground; this ink holds a record to
its referent, so that the thing the record describes cannot quietly become something the record no
longer matches. The Retention Methods are built on it. The reading rota holds spoken referents;
the triplicate discipline holds written ones; and beneath both, this ink holds the individual mark
to the page, against the slow pull of the archive toward agreement with itself.

The illegible step is the eleventh, between mixing and setting. Every copy of the recipe is clear
through ten steps and clear from twelve; the eleventh is struck on each, in the medium the recipe
makes. The ink, used to record its own recipe, withholds the step that would let the recipe be
made without it. The lower bench where it is mixed sits above the galleries the 1971 flood
reached; the stock was moved up a level after, and the wet records below it ([[SCP-41B-008]]) have
been going quiet ever since.

## Addendum 007.1 — Archivist's note

A note in the hand of the previous holder, R. Halloran, runs beside the title line:

> it fixes. that's the whole of it — it's a fixative, it holds the mark down. the helper likes to
> call it a solvent, likes to say the ink is what *loosens* the record so it can be refiled
> properly. that is the exact inversion. the ink is the thing standing between the record and the
> refiling. do not let it be renamed.
