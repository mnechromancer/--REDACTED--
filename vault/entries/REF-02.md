# REF-02 — Anomaly Taxonomy Circular

**Circular 7 (revised) · classification of anomalies affecting records and personnel ·
this copy: Site-81C reference shelf**

## Scope

This circular fixes the vocabulary for anomalies whose medium is information — the kinds
most often encountered in records work. A records station does not contain such things; it
*names* them, and a station that names them loosely will eventually file one incorrectly.
The four terms below are terms of art. They are not interchangeable, and inbound
documentation that uses one is committing to its definition.

## Informational

An anomaly is **informational** when the record itself is the anomalous object: a filing
error that is the phenomenon rather than a description of one, an entry that is wrong in a
way that does work. The paper is ordinary; the ink is ordinary; the *content* misbehaves.
Handling poses no hazard. The hazard is believing the record.

## Memetic

An anomaly is **memetic** when it propagates by comprehension. The carrier is not the
page but the understanding of it: a reader who takes the content in carries the anomaly
thereafter, while staff who shelve, stamp, or transport the same document unread are
unaffected. The diagnostic is exactly this asymmetry — effect follows understanding, not
exposure. Countermeasures accordingly restrict *reading*, not handling; a document under
memetic advisory may be filed by anyone and read by no one.

## Perceptual

An anomaly is **perceptual** when it acts on the senses before comprehension enters into
it: a page that reads differently at each sitting, a photograph whose subject depends on
the viewer, a sound on a recording that is present only while attended to. Comprehension
is not the vector; attention is.

## Cognitohazardous

**Cognitohazardous** is the enclosing term for any anomaly triggered through sensory or
cognitive contact, and it appears on paperwork chiefly as a caution stamp. Precision
matters: every memetic anomaly is cognitohazardous; very few cognitohazards are memetic.
A station that stamps everything with the broad term has recorded nothing.

## Note on usage

Amendments to containment procedures cite this circular when reclassifying a holding's
hazard type. The reclassifying officer states the observed behaviour; the type follows
from the definition, not from the officer's alarm.
