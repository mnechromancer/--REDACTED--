# REF-05 — Standard Forms Compendium (blank)

**Foundation standard forms, records series · blank reference set · this copy: Site-81C
reference shelf · DO NOT FILL — REFERENCE ONLY**

## Purpose

Every standard form is a fixed skeleton: the fields do not move, the field order does not
vary, and what kind of value each field takes is set by the form, not the filer. A reader
who knows the blank can read a damaged, incomplete, or obscured copy by its bones — the
form fixes what *kind* of thing each entry must be, which is often most of what a missing
entry was. The blanks below are the forms most common in inbound records traffic.

## Form 41-T — Intake Transfer Slip

The cover document of any consignment between sites. Fields, in order:

1. ORIGINATING SITE
2. RECEIVING STATION
3. CONSIGNMENT NUMBER
4. MANIFEST (designations of all records enclosed, one per line)
5. SCHEDULE (delivery and cancellation times, where a cancellation applies)
6. CLASS OF RECORD (the object class governing the consignment — see the primer)
7. POSTURE DURING TRANSIT (free text, written by the escorting hand)
8. COUNTERSIGNATURE (liaison of the parent site, where one answers)

Field 7 is free text and field 6 is a single word; where field 6 is unreadable, field 7
usually still says what it was.

## Form 41-P — Personnel Action Memorandum

Records an appointment, transfer, or separation. Fields: ACTION · NAME AND POST ·
EFFECTIVE DATE · AUTHORITY (the signing officer) · REMARKS. The NAME entry repeats
nowhere else on the form; a 41-P with its name obscured must be recovered from the
directory of the issuing site, which is why receiving stations keep one.

## Form 41-C — Containment Amendment

Amends the special containment procedures of a single holding. Fields: HOLDING ·
SECTION AMENDED · OBSERVED BEHAVIOUR (free text) · HAZARD TYPE OF RECORD (a term of
art — see the taxonomy circular) · EFFECTIVE READING. The form requires the observed
behaviour to be stated *before* the type, so that the type can be checked against it.

## Form 41-R — Retention Order

Fixes the keeping of an enumerated lot. Fields: LOT (designations bound by the order) ·
COPYING DISCIPLINE · DISTRIBUTION · REVIEW CYCLE · ISSUING OFFICER. The discipline field
takes the name of a standard practice from the records manual, never a description.
