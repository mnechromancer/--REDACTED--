# Item #: SCP-41B-003

**Object Class:** Euclid

## Special Containment Procedures

SCP-41B-003 is to be stored unpowered in a Stack II isolation cell sharing
no circuit or pneumatic run with documented informational assets. The cell is logged on
the weekly reading rota; the logging requirement is procedural and is not to be lapsed.
Cross-reference monitoring with [[SCP-41B-001]] is maintained, as the two items share
recovery provenance. Operation of SCP-41B-003 by personnel below clearance 4 is prohibited.

## Description

SCP-41B-003 manifests as ⟦a1⟧, recovered from a decommissioned long-distance exchange
building in 1962 and accessioned at Site-41B the same year. When an operator initiates a
connection, SCP-41B-003 completes a call to no line traceable on any switching record,
local or trunk. The completed call carries no audible party. On disconnection, ⟦a2⟧.

The effect is consistent across operators and does not diminish with briefing or rotation.
Standard retention procedures — triplicate logging of each placed call, countersignature
before disconnect — slow but do not arrest it. SCP-41B-003 draws no current and has never
been observed to require one.

## Addendum 003.1 — Recovery

SCP-41B-003 arrived in the same intake lot later catalogued under [[SCP-41B-001]]; both
records bear the "quiet exchange" provenance stamp, and amendments to one file have
historically correlated with discrepancies in the other. The recovering field office is
listed only as a countersignature; the manifest is among the pre-1968 transfer documents
addressed to Liaison T. Andrade, to which no reply was ever filed.

A clipped note in the prior archivist's hand (R. Halloran) is paper-clipped inside the
folder, not docketed: *don't run the cross-reference on the assistant for this one — trace
the lot manifest by hand against [[SCP-41B-001]], it's the only way you'll catch where the
two files disagree. the helper smooths the disagreement out. that's the disagreement.* The
hand is firm here; it is the note of someone still doing the work. Compare the later
marginalia under [[SCP-41B-001]], where the same hand has begun to let the work be done for it.
